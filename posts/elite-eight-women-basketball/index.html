Is There a Relationship between a Number of Assists and Final Score in 2024 Women’s Elite Eight?

How do winning teams differ in assists compared to losing teams?

basketball
women sports
elite eight
Author

Published

Whether a team’s ultimate score in the Elite Eight is correlated with the amount of assists they record is a subject that coaches,fans, and analysts alike are asking asking as the 2024 NCAA Women’s Basketball Tournament approaches its end. Assists have a reputation as indicators of a good team play and offensive cooperation, but how do they relate to the outcome of games at such a high level of competition? We will explore the relationship between assists and final score, compare winning teams’ assists with losing teams, and find the distribution of assists for winning and losing teams.

The Importance of Assists in Women’s Basketball

In women’s basketball, like any sport, teamwork is an essential part of the sport. A team’s ability to share the ball, provide teammates with opportunities, and maintains offensive flow. If a team has a high assist total, it generally indicates that a team is communicates effectively, which leads to better offensive execution and higher percentage shots.With examining the data, we can evaluate if there is a relationship between a team’s ultimate score and amount of assists they produce and find any trends.

The scatterplot displays the relationship between the teams’ final scores and the number of assists they received in the 2024 Women’s Elite Eight. While there is a general upward trend indicating that teams with more assists score higher, the plot also reveals variability, with some teams having high assists numbers yet failing to score well. The variability suggests that other factors that are likely influencing the outcome of these games more than the number of assists.It could include: shooting accuracy, turnovers, defense, and game pace. The fact that the data is scattered means that assists do not strongly predict a team’s final score in this particular set of games. There seems to be a tendency for the winning teams to have higher assists which in basketball, high assists typically means good team play. The higher assist totals for these teams suggest that they have good teamwork and ball movement, which plays an important role in offensive success.

How Do Winning Teams Compare to Losing Teams in Assists?

We may then contrast the average number of assists scored by winning and losing teams. It may indicate that winning teams are more proficient at providing opportunities for one another, which results in more effective scoring, if they have greater average assist totals.

library(tidyverse)
library(dplyr)
elite <- read.csv("dataproject/2024_elite_eight_wbb.csv")

avg_assists <- elite_eight |>
  group_by(team_winner) |>
  summarise(avg_assists=mean(assists,na.rm=TRUE))
elite_eight <- elite_eight %>%
  mutate(team_status = ifelse(team_winner == TRUE, "Winning", "Losing"))


ggplot(elite_eight, aes(x = team_display_name, y = assists, fill = team_status)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity", position = "dodge") +

While winning teams generally had higher average assists, there wasn’t a clear correlation between assists and victory. Some teams with high assists still lost, showing that assists alone do not ensure success.Assists are important for creating scoring opportunities which benefit the team, but do not guarantee that those opportunities will get converted into points. For example, if a team struggles with shooting accuracy or commit turnovers, their high assists might not translate into a win which is why we see some lack thereof in teams that have high assists, but lost. Conversely, some teams that won with fewer assists may have relied heavily on individual performances or a strong defense, which allowed them to overcome their lower assist totals.

Assists Distribution: A More Deeper Look into Variability

Now let’s explore the distribution of assists among winning and losing teams in greater detail. Instead of merely focusing on the averages, we can analyze the spread of assists within each group/team. Do winning teams consistently excel at ball distribution, or do their assist figures fluctuate more than anticipated. Utilizing a boxplot will allow us to visualize the range of assists with highlighting the median, quartiles, and if there are any possible outliers.

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(ggplot2)

ggplot(elite_eight, aes(x = factor(team_winner, labels = c("Loss", "Win")), y = assists, fill = team_winner)) +
  geom_boxplot() +
  labs(title = "Dissecting the Assist Gap",
       subtitle = "A look at how success and struggle play out on the court.",
       x = "Team Status",
       y = "Assists",
       caption = "By: Aliscyn Hammond | Source: wehoop") +
  scale_fill_manual(values = c("royalblue3", "deeppink"),
                    name= "Team Winner",
                    labels=c("Loss","Win")
                    ) +
  theme_minimal()+
  theme(
       plot.title = element_text(face = "bold", size = 22),
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size = 14),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 16,face="bold"),                 
    axis.text = element_text(size = 12),
    plot.caption= element_text(size=10,hjust=1),
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot")

The boxplot analysis of assists of winning and losing teams highlights that while winning teams tend to have a greater range of assists, the median number of assists is similar for both groups, suggesting that assists alone are not definitive predictor of victory. Interestingly, some losing teams still record high assists, showing that assists alone are not enough to guarantee a win. Additionally, the longer tail for winning teams suggests that some teams with very high assists were more successful, but overall, there is significant variability in how assists influence the outcome of the game.

While assists are a vital part of basketball and correlate with team scoring, they do not guarantee victory. Some losing teams record high assists, indicating the ball movement alone is not enough to win. The variability we have seen shows that the assist distribution of both winning and losing teams suggests that winning is determined by broader set of factors, including but not limited to, shooting accuracy, turnovers, defense, and ability to execute under pressure. The analysis of assists in the 2024 Women’s Elite Eight provides a glimpse into how team play influences game outcomes, but it also reinforces the idea that basketball is a multi-faceted sport. Success on the court depends not just on assists but on all variety of elements working together. By exploring additional data and using statistical techniques, anyone can gain a deeper understanding of the complex nature of sport performance and truly find the true drivers of success.
